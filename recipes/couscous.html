Couscous Recipe

Home

Description

Moroccan couscous is a traditional and comforting dish made of steamed semolina grains served with a rich, slow-cooked stew of meat and vegetables. Known for its delicate texture and layered flavors, it combines warm spices with tender ingredients to create a balanced and satisfying meal. Often enjoyed on Fridays or during family gatherings, couscous is as much about the experience of sharing as it is about the food itself.

Ingredients

  • Couscous (semolina grains)
  • Lamb or chicken (cut into pieces)
  • Onion (chopped)
  • Garlic cloves (minced)
  • Olive oil
  • Chickpeas (cooked or canned)
  • Carrots (peeled and cut into large pieces)
  • Zucchini (cut into large pieces)
  • Turnips (peeled and halved)
  • Pumpkin or squash (cut into chunks)
  • Tomatoes (grated or finely chopped)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Ground ginger
  • Turmeric
  • Cinnamon stick
  • Saffron threads (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley (tied together or chopped)
  • Water or broth

Steps

  1. Start by heating olive oil in a large pot. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, then add the garlic and cook briefly.
  2. Add the meat pieces and brown them lightly on all sides. Stir in the spices, including salt, pepper, ginger, turmeric, and a pinch of saffron if using.
  3. Add the tomatoes, chickpeas, fresh herbs, and enough water or broth to cover everything. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer.
  4. Add the harder vegetables first, such as carrots and turnips, and let them cook until they begin to soften. Then add the zucchini and pumpkin, continuing to simmer until all vegetables are tender and the broth is flavorful.
  5. While the stew is cooking, prepare the couscous by moistening it lightly with water and a bit of olive oil, then fluffing it with your hands to separate the grains. Steam the couscous until light and fluffy, repeating the steaming process if needed for a finer texture.
  6. Once everything is ready, place the couscous on a large serving dish and shape it into a mound. Create a well in the center and arrange the meat and vegetables on top.
  7. Ladle the broth over the dish or serve it on the side, and enjoy while warm.
