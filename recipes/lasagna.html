Lasagna Recipe

Home

Description

A classic lasagna is a rich, comforting Italian dish made by layering tender pasta sheets with a hearty meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta mixture, and melted cheese. This version balances savory ground beef with a slow-simmered tomato sauce, giving it depth and warmth in every bite. Perfect for family dinners or gatherings, lasagna is one of those dishes that tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets (dried or fresh)
  • Ground beef
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic cloves (minced)
  • Olive oil
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Ricotta cheese (or béchamel sauce)
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Egg (optional, for ricotta mixture)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, chopped)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce by heating olive oil in a pan, then sauté the chopped onion until soft. Add the garlic and cook briefly before adding the ground beef. Cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  2. Stir in the tomato sauce and tomato paste, then season with salt, pepper, oregano, and basil. Let the sauce simmer on low heat for 20–30 minutes to develop flavor.
  3. If using ricotta, mix it in a bowl with an egg, a pinch of salt, and chopped parsley until smooth.
  4. Preheat the oven and prepare a baking dish. Start layering by spreading a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom. Add a layer of lasagna sheets, followed by more sauce, then ricotta (or béchamel), and a sprinkle of mozzarella.
  5. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a top layer of sauce and a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Bake in the oven until the top is golden and bubbling.
  7. Remove from the oven and let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
